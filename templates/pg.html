Am I Pregnant?>

If you have had sexual intercourse (sexual interaction between a man’s erect penis and woman’s vagina) recently without using protection there is a chance that you may be pregnant.

During sexual intercourse a man ejaculates semen (or cum) which is a mixture of sperm cells and other fluids. After ejaculation the millions of sperm cells are released and swim towards the egg. However only one sperm cell can fertilize the egg in order for pregnancy to happen. Although the odds seem small it is more common than you think. The chances of pregnancy are between 15% and 25% each month however there are factors that increase those chances like:

  • Age: younger women, specifically under 30 years older have a higher chance of getting pregnant.
  • Frequency of sex
  • Illnesses

Pregnancy officially starts when a fertilized egg implants in the lining of the uterus. It takes up to 2-3 weeks after sex for pregnancy to happen. It is possible for a woman to get pregnant while she is on her period. Although the chances are lower than regularly, it is still very possible, especially after unprotected sex. Thus “period sex” may not be as safe as suspected. A man’s sperm can live inside a woman for up to 72 hours after ejaculation. Toward the end of your period, your chances of becoming pregnant will increase.

Here are the most common signs of pregnancy:

  • Missed period
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Breast Tenderness and Enlargement
  • Fatigue
  • Needing to pee frequently
  • Cravings, especially unusual ones like combinations of strange foods.

A late period can be a sign of pregnancy. If your period is about a week late and you are experiencing any of the above symptoms you can take a pregnancy test to be sure.

Infertility

Infertility is defined as trying to get pregnant with frequent, unprotected sex for at least a year with no success.

Infertility results from female factors about one-third of the time and both female and male factors about one-third of the time. The cause is either unknown or a combination of male and female factors in the remaining cases.

Female infertility causes can be difficult to diagnose. There are many treatments, depending on the infertility cause.

Symptoms

The main symptom of infertility is the inability to get pregnant. A menstrual cycle that's too long (35 days or more), too short (less than 21 days), irregular or absent can mean that you're not ovulating. There might be no other signs or symptoms.

When to see a doctor

When to seek help can depend on your age:

  • Up to age 35, most doctors recommend trying to get pregnant for at least a year before testing or treatment.
  • If you're between 35 and 40, discuss your concerns with your doctor after six months of trying.
  • If you're older than 40, your doctor might suggest testing or treatment right away.

    • Your doctor might also want to begin testing or treatment right away if you or your partner has known fertility problems, or if you have a history of irregular or painful periods, pelvic inflammatory disease, repeated miscarriages, cancer treatment, or endometriosis.
